Honest Answers

The questions every smart pool buyer should ask, before they hire anyone.

How much does a custom pool cost in St. Johns County?

Most of the custom pools we build run between $65,000 and $150,000+, depending on size, shape, features (tanning ledge, spa, waterfall, lighting), decking, and screen enclosure. We give you a detailed, itemized written quote after your free consultation. No "ballpark." No surprises later. If financing makes sense, we'll walk you through the Lyon Financial options that fit your budget.

Is the consultation really free? What's the catch?

There's no catch. The consultation costs you nothing, takes about 25 minutes in your yard, and you owe us nothing afterward. We've built our reputation in St. Augustine one honest conversation at a time, and that's how we want to keep building it.

Do you handle the permits and county inspections?

Yes, all of it. As a State Certified Pool Contractor (CPC-056960), we pull every permit, schedule every inspection, and handle every code requirement, including coastal-zone and historic-area restrictions when they apply. You stay out of the county office. We stay in it.

What's actually covered by your warranty? Any "cosmetic" loopholes?

The structure, plumbing, and electrical work are covered. The plaster is covered (real coverage, not the "cosmetic mottling doesn't count" trick). The equipment carries the manufacturer warranty plus our labor. If something fails because of how we built it, we fix it. That's it. We hand you the full warranty in writing before you ever sign a contract.

How long does it take to build a custom pool, start to finish?

Most projects take 4-8 weeks depending on complexity. Pool installations average 4-6 weeks, while complete outdoor living spaces with kitchens and structures can take 6-8 weeks. We provide a detailed timeline during your consultation. Weather permitting - we work year-round in Florida's climate.

How do I know you won't run out of money or disappear before my pool is finished?

It's a fair question, and honestly it's the fear behind most of the others. Here's the straight answer: we've been building pools in St. Johns County for more than 15 years, and we're still here because we finish what we start. Florida Coastal Contractors is a State Certified, licensed, bonded, and insured company (CPC-056960 and CBC-1263763), so you're protected by more than a handshake. We're local and family-owned with our name on the line in this community, not a crew chasing the next deposit. You also pay as the work gets completed, phase by phase, never a large sum upfront for work we haven't done yet. Want proof? Ask us for homeowners we built for years ago. The fact that they still recommend us is the real answer.

How do I know you won't cut corners underground where I'll never see it?

This is exactly where bad builders cut corners, because they're betting you'll never see it. We build the opposite way. Every pool we build is inspected by St. Johns County at each critical phase: the steel and rebar before the shell is poured, the plumbing while it's pressure tested, the electrical and bonding, and the final. Those inspections happen whether you watch or not, and you're welcome to be there for every one. We also walk you through the equipment and materials before they go in the ground, not after. As a State Certified Pool Contractor (CPC-056960), the parts you'll never see are held to the same standard as the tile you will. Built once, built right, because we're the ones who answer for it 15 years from now.

Can I see a pool you built before I commit?

Yes. We'll arrange visits to recently-completed pools right here in St. Johns County. Walk the deck, see the plaster up close, talk to the homeowner. Our reputation in this town is everything to us, so we make sure you can verify it firsthand.

What kind of pool do you build, concrete, fiberglass, or vinyl?

We build custom concrete (gunite) pools, the strongest and most flexible option for fully custom shapes, depths, tanning ledges, and spas. Unlike a pre-molded fiberglass shell or a vinyl liner, gunite lets us match your exact design instead of forcing your yard to fit a stock mold, and it's engineered to hold up to Florida's coquina soil and salt air. During your consultation we'll walk you through the trade-offs honestly, so you choose what's right for your yard and budget, not what's easiest for us to pour.

Once the work starts, how will you keep me updated, and will you actually answer my calls?

Communication is where most pool builds fall apart, so we built ours around it. From day one you get your project manager's direct cell phone, not a main office line or a ticket system. You call or text the person actually running your build, and we pick up. You also get regular progress updates, so you always know what phase you're in and what's coming next instead of wondering if you've been forgotten. It's the same team from design to the final walk-through, so the person who answers in week ten is the same one who answered in week one. If we ever go quiet on you, that's on us, and it's not how we've earned 15 years of referrals in this town.

What will my yard, driveway, and property look like during and after the build?

For a few months your backyard is our jobsite, and how we treat it says everything about how we work. We protect your driveway and access path before the heavy equipment ever rolls in, and we clean the site at the end of every workday instead of leaving trash, cigarette butts, and material scraps for you to look at. We're careful around your landscaping, your fencing, and your neighbors. The goal is simple: your driveway looks the same on day 90 as it did on day 1, and the only thing that changed about your yard is the resort that's now in it. If our crew ever damages something, we own it and we fix it. No finger-pointing, no "that was already like that."
